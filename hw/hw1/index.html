A software development life cycle (SDLC) (also known as an application development life cycle, also known as a systems development life cycle) is a term used in software engineering, systems engineering, and information systems to describe the a process of planning, creating, testing, and finally deploying software. Although it is possible to break the process down into distinct general stages, (as the diagram at left shows) there are many varying models of SDLCs. Thus, there are many different methodologies used for planning and creating functional software. In this brief overview of SDLCs in general, we will examine four distinct models of SDLC commonly in use at present: the agile, lean, iterative, and waterfall models.
