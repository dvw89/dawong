Iterative and incremental development focuses on devloping a program through repeated cycles (iterations) broken into smaller portions (increments), allowing programmers to learn from earlier stages of development and use of the software. This model was created mostly to address the inefficiencies in the waterfall model of development.







Contrasts with the waterfall model

Instead of having the customer involved in only one stage of the development (namely the planning stage), iterative development encourages the customer to continually review the progress on the program after each cycle, increasing the developers' understanding of what the customer wants while also allowing the developers to learn more after each cycle. Also, less manpower is needed than with the waterfall model. Another advantage is that the user is presented with a fundamental working project much faster than with waterfall.
